## Design Context

### Users
TripIn serves people who record and browse travel routes and everyday city movement. They open the product first as a community feed, then go deeper into route detail or editing when a post catches their attention. The feed is for lightweight discovery and social consumption, not for immediate map work.

### Brand Personality
Light, restrained, and lifestyle-oriented. The interface should feel calm and contemporary rather than editorial, dashboard-like, or aggressively technical. It should feel like a real social product with route intelligence inside it.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a predominantly white visual system with near-black primary text and soft gray metadata. The feed should read closer to Instagram than to a travel dashboard. Default media framing should assume phone-shot portrait photography. Route maps stay inside each post, but they act as a compressed supporting layer under the photo rather than as the hero. Avoid yellow-brown paper moods, boxed cards, rounded modules, thin separator scaffolding, and decorative utility chrome.

### Design Principles
1. Community feed first, map tooling second.
2. Separate posts with whitespace, scale, and rhythm instead of borders or card shells.
3. Keep every feed item compact enough to fit comfortably inside a desktop viewport.
4. Preserve route identity in-feed by keeping a visible route map in every post.
5. Let avatars stay circular, but keep the rest of the feed sharp-edged and quiet.
